My Classroom Observations

Over the past few weeks, I've had the privilege of observing classes at City Central Elementary School every Friday. This experience has provided me with invaluable insights into the dynamics of a classroom, the role of the teacher, and the diverse learning styles of young students.

The Learners

The students, especially the grade 5 students at City Central Elementary School are a bright and enthusiastic group of learners. They are very eager to learn and participate in class discussions. Their respectful behavior towards their peers and teacher, Mr. Momongan, fosters a positive and conducive learning environment. I was also allowed to observe a different class and grade level. There, I realized that they have basic and common with the other class- they are well- behave and obedient. It's evident that they feel safe and valued in their classroom.

The Teacher

Mr. Momongan, the Grade 5 teacher, is a dedicated and skilled educator. His firm yet fair approach to classroom management ensures discipline without stifling creativity. His enthusiasm for the subject matter is contagious, keeping students engaged and motivated. He effectively uses a variety of teaching strategies, such as direct instruction, group work, and independent activities, to cater to different learning styles. His ability to create a positive and supportive learning environment is commendable.

The Learning Environment

The classroom environment is clean, well-organized, and visually stimulating. The walls are adorned with colorful displays of student work, creating an inspiring and engaging atmosphere. The seating arrangement promotes collaboration and active participation. The availability of diverse instructional materials, such as textbooks, workbooks, and manipulatives, enhances the learning experience.

Instructional Materials

The instructional materials used in the classroom are age-appropriate, relevant, and aligned with the curriculum. Textbooks and workbooks provide the core content, while supplementary materials, such as worksheets, flashcards, and real-world objects, reinforce learning. The use of technology, such as interactive whiteboards, adds a modern dimension to the lessons.

The Teaching-Learning Process

Mr. Momongan employs a variety of teaching strategies to cater to different learning styles. His lessons are well-structured, with clear objectives and engaging activities. He skillfully uses questioning techniques to promote critical thinking and problem-solving skills. Students are actively involved in discussions, ask questions, and share their ideas. The teacher effectively uses a combination of whole- class instruction, small-group work, and individual activities to differentiate instruction.

Assessing Student Learning

Mr. Momongan employs a variety of assessment strategies to monitor student progress. Formative assessments, such as quizzes, exit slips, and class discussions, provide immediate feedback and inform instruction. Summative assessments, such as tests and projects, evaluate student learning at the end of a unit. He also uses observation and anecdotal records to assess students' social and emotional development.

Overall, my observations at City Central Elementary School have been a truly enriching experience. The dedication of the teachers and the enthusiasm of the students have left a lasting impression on me. I am grateful for the opportunity to witness firsthand the power of effective teaching and learning.

A Transformative Learning Experience

Field Study 1 was a pivotal experience that significantly shaped my understanding of the teaching profession. Observing seasoned educators like Mr. Momongan at City Central Elementary School provided invaluable insights into classroom dynamics, instructional strategies, and student engagement.

Witnessing firsthand the diverse learning styles of young learners and the innovative ways teachers cater to individual needs was truly inspiring. It ignited a passion within me to create engaging and inclusive learning environments that foster both academic and personal growth.

Through reflective practice, I was able to critically analyze my observations and identify areas for improvement in my own future teaching practice. The opportunity to connect theoretical knowledge with practical experience solidified my understanding of the complex interplay between the teacher, the student, and the curriculum.

As I embark on my journey as a pre-service teacher, I am grateful for the foundation laid by FS 1. It has equipped me with the necessary tools to become a compassionate and effective educator, eager to inspire and empower the next generation of learners.
